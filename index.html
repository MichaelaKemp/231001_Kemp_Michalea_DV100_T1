LINKIN PARK


Linkin Park is an American rock band that formed in 1996 in Agoura Hills, California. The band's original lineup consisted of Mike Shinoda (vocals, guitar, keyboards), Brad Delson (guitar), Rob Bourdon (drums), Dave "Phoenix" Farrell (bass), Joe Hahn (turntables, samples), and the late Chester Bennington (vocals). Linkin Park gained international fame with their debut album "Hybrid Theory" in 2000, which featured the hit singles "One Step Closer" and "Crawling." The album went on to become one of the best-selling albums of the 21st century, with over 27 million copies sold worldwide. The band continued to release successful albums, including "Meteora" (2003), "Minutes to Midnight" (2007), "A Thousand Suns" (2010), "Living Things" (2012), and "One More Light" (2017). Linkin Park's music has been described as a blend of nu-metal, alternative rock, and electronic music. Tragically, Chester Bennington passed away on July 20, 2017, and the band has not released any new music since then. However, Linkin Park remains a popular and influential band, and their music continues to resonate with fans around the world.
